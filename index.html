Discrete Math Example

Permutations of a Standard Card Deck

It is theorized that each time a deck of cards is shuffled, the resulting combination of cards is unique in the history of the world, and that each time a deck is shuffled, that order has never occurred before. In a deck of 54 cards (one that includes two Jokers), each combination is one out of 230,843,697,339,241,380,472,092,742,683,027,581,083,278,564,571,807,941,132,288,000,000,000,000 possible combinations!
